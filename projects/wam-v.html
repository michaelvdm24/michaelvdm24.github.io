WAM-V Project Overview

During my tenure at Marine Advanced Robotics, I led the electrical controls design for the WAM-V project, focusing on enhancing its operational capabilities through the integration of advanced technologies. My key contributions included designing a GPS broadcasting system with USB-C power delivery and PoE for improved communication and developing a high-precision winch system for SVP Sonar.

GPS, Radio, and USB-C Incorporated Follow Box

Designed a GPS broadcasting system featuring USB-C power delivery protocols and PoE for enhanced radio communication. Engineered a robust UDP-based protocol to transmit real-time GPS data to user laptops for optimizing navigation solutions.

Sound Velocity Profile Winch and Echosounder

Engineered a high-precision winch system for SVP Sonar, integrating quadrature encoders and brushless DC motor controls. Developed a robust UDP communication protocol between machines, thoroughly tested through a Python-based API and GUI.

Linear Actuator Folding Mechanism for WAM-V 22'

Designed a PCB that integrates relays, timed control, digital feedback, override switches, and UDP communications to manage the robot's folding and locking mechanisms. This innovation significantly enhanced the functionality and adaptability of the WAM-V's operational modes.

Hemisphere Integration on Raspberry Pi

Optimized System on Chip (SoC) integration to meet critical specifications for power efficiency and enhanced navigation in WAM-V boats. Integrated SoC subsystems to parse NMEA data and distribute GPS information to various microcontrollers via serial communications, improving the accuracy and reliability of navigational systems.
